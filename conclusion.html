Conclusion

To summarize, the human workforce and artificial intelligence should not eliminate each other, instead, they should work together to create a better form of result. P. Uria-Recio [18] T. Livne, "Why AI and Humans Are Stronger Together Than Apart," 2019. [Online]. Available: https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/329099 founded that process-oriented skills require 80% of machine skills, cross-functional reasoning skills require 80% of human skills, and quantitative reasoning skills require both 50% of machine skills and 50% of human skills. So, artificial intelligence does not automate jobs, it automates certain skills in jobs. It is concluded that artificial intelligence will not replace the human workforce. They should work together to benefit everyone in multiple aspects.
